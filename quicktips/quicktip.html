Heart Attack

If you suspect someone is having a heart attack:

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Make the person sit down, rest, and try to keep calm.
  • Loosen tight clothing, especially around the neck.
  • Help them take their medication, like nitroglycerin.
  • If unconscious, start CPR if trained.

Burns

For minor burns:

  • Cool the burn with cool (not cold) water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover with a sterile, non-adhesive bandage.
  • Avoid applying ice, butter, or ointments.
  • Seek medical attention for serious burns.

Fractures

If you suspect a fracture:

  • Immobilize the injured area; avoid moving the person.
  • Apply a splint if trained.
  • Use ice packs to reduce swelling.
  • Keep the person still and calm until help arrives.

Choking

If someone is choking and unable to cough:

  • Ask if they can speak.
  • Perform the Heimlich maneuver.
  • If unconscious, start CPR immediately.
  • Seek emergency help immediately.

Severe Bleeding

If someone is bleeding heavily:

  • Apply firm pressure to the wound.
  • Keep the injured part elevated.
  • Do not remove embedded objects; apply pressure around them.
  • Seek emergency help immediately.

Asthma Attack

If someone is having an asthma attack:

  • Help them use their inhaler or medication as prescribed.
  • Encourage them to sit upright and stay calm.
  • If symptoms persist or worsen, seek emergency medical help.

Shock

If someone is in shock:

  • Lay them down on their back.
  • Keep them warm with a blanket.
  • Elevate their legs slightly if possible.
  • Do not give them anything to eat or drink.
  • Seek emergency medical help immediately.

Heatstroke

If someone is experiencing heatstroke:

  • Move them to a cooler place immediately.
  • Apply cool, damp cloths to their skin.
  • Offer fluids if they are conscious and able to drink.
  • Seek medical help urgently as heatstroke can be life-threatening.

Poisoning

If you suspect someone has been poisoned:

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless told to do so by a professional.
  • If the poison is on the skin, rinse with water for 15-20 minutes.
  • Keep the person still and calm.

Electric Shock

For someone suffering from electric shock:

  • Do not touch them if they are still in contact with the source.
  • Turn off the source of electricity if possible.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Check for breathing and heartbeat; start CPR if necessary.

Frostbite

For someone with frostbite:

  • Move them to a warmer place immediately.
  • Immerse the affected area in warm (not hot) water.
  • Do not rub the frostbitten area, as it can cause damage.
  • Seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Seizures

If someone is having a seizure:

  • Stay calm and ensure the person's safety.
  • Clear the area of objects that could cause injury.
  • Protect their head with a soft item.
  • Time the seizure; call emergency services if it lasts more than 5 minutes.
  • Do not restrain them or put anything in their mouth.
  • Stay with them until they are fully awake.
